## Talent Level: The Honest Version

These are real differentiators between good engineers and research-grade contributors:

### What You Likely Have

- **Persistence** — You've committed to a serious roadmap. Most people don't.
- **Structured thinking** — You've planned this carefully; that's a genuine signal.
- **Growth orientation** — You're reading this assessment, which means you can handle uncomfortable truths.

### What You Almost Certainly Lack Right Now

| Gap | Why It Matters | How Long to Close |
|---|---|---|
| **Mathematical maturity** | Research papers assume calculus, linear algebra, probability fluency | 6–12 months of deliberate practice |
| **Debugging deep models** | When your transformer doesn't converge, you need intuitions to fix it | Develops through Project 2–3 failures |
| **Paper writing** | Knowing how to communicate research is a separate skill from doing it | 3–6 months of writing practice |
| **Independent problem formulation** | The hardest research skill: deciding *what* to work on | Develops over years |
| **Speed** | Top researchers move fast; first iterations should take hours, not weeks | Experience only |

### The Uncomfortable Truth About ARC-AGI

ARC-AGI is one of the hardest benchmarks in AI. The current best open-source solutions were built by teams with:
- 5–15 years of ML experience
- PhD-level mathematical background
- Months of full-time engineering effort

You are building toward this. You will not beat SOTA with your first attempt. You may not beat SOTA at all in 8 months. **That is okay.** The goal is:
1. Understand *why* current approaches work (or don't)
2. Make a meaningful contribution (even 5% improvement on a specific ARC category)
3. Build a portfolio that demonstrates genuine depth

## What Separates Good from Great

The people who succeed at this roadmap and go on to do real research share these traits:
1. **They work with the code, not just the text.** Every concept they read, they implement.
2. **They fail in public.** They put broken code on GitHub. They write blog posts about what didn't work.
3. **They find communities.** ARC Discord, ML Twitter/X, paper reading groups.
4. **They compound slowly.** Month 1 feels like nothing. Month 8 feels like a step change.
5. **They ask better questions, not more questions.** Quality of engagement > quantity of effort.

**None of these require talent. All of them require discipline.**
